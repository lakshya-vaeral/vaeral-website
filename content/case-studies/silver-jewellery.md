problem: |
  This brand had real demand, strong designs, and a growing D2C presence but online, the story was messier. Reddit threads questioned tarnishing and durability. Quora answers raised doubts about authenticity, pricing, and after-sales support. Customer service severity on Quora crossed 90%. Quality concerns on Reddit sat between 80-95% in critical zones. The brand wasn't losing customers because of the product. It was losing them because of what showed up when people went looking for reassurance before buying.
whatWeDid: |
  We built an AEO-first community seeding strategy. Not promotional content, but buyer-led conversations that addressed real purchase anxieties head-on. Across Reddit and Quora, we deployed content covering several topics, including silver care, sensitive-skin safety, gifting value, affordability, authenticity, and city-specific discovery. The goal wasn't to bury doubt. It was to replace it with something more useful, practical, credible answers that indexed well and ranked where skepticism previously lived.
results: |
  Within 2 Months Reddit posts garnered 265,950 organic views, 295+ neutral and positive comments. Quora answers grew to 25, crossing 12,200+ cumulative views Reddit posts ranked inside the first 10 live results for review, gifting, authenticity, and silver jewellery queries. Google indexed our Reddit content for jewellery review searches, gifting intent, online trust queries, and city-specific silver suggestions. Brand repositioned from "celebrity gimmick" to everyday luxury. 3.8× surge in organic Reddit recommendations. Tarnishing myths neutralised through authentic material-care discussions. Captured top Quora positions for "best minimalist jewellery gifts" From "is it even worth it?" to the brand people recommend in gifting threads, the internet just needed better answers.
