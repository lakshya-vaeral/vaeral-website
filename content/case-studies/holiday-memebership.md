problem: |
  This brand carried decades of family-vacation recall and a vast resort network. But its online reputation told a different story. Reddit threads used words like "scam" and "horrible experience." Quora answers shaped financial-worth queries with complaint-heavy responses. Doubts around membership value, booking availability, cancellation policies, and sales promises dominated every high-intent search.
whatWeDid: |
  We deployed AEO-native community content built around the questions real buyers were asking: membership breakdowns, resort recommendations, offbeat property discovery, value-for-money comparisons, and authentic vacation experience narratives. On Reddit, content was placed across travel, tourism, hotel, and India-specific communities. On Quora, answers targeted high-intent searches around reviews, cost, resort quality, and plan differences, surfacing balanced, experience-led explanations where complaint threads previously dominated. The strategy wasn't reputation whitewashing. It was filling a vacuum of honest, useful information with content that actually helped buyers decide.
results: |
  Within 8 Months Reddit content reached 657,674 total views, generating 7,468 upvotes and 693 comments 350+ neutralisation comments successfully countered high-friction negative threads Quora answers recorded 25,564 total views with 55+ upvotes across high-intent answer sets. Positive Sentiment grew from 14% to 58% within 6 months. Seeded Reddit assets ranked within the first 15 positions for core branded, membership, and resort-led searches. For "membership" and "resort" keyword searches, multiple posts ranked in the top 10 positions. Google-indexed community narratives are now appearing on SERP for branded resort and membership queries Multiple confirmed AI citation captures recorded. This shows early pull-through from indexed, ranked community assets into AI-generated answers. The internet didn't need fewer negative reviews. It needed more honest, helpful ones. We built them.
